About the James Webb Space Telescope

The James Webb Space Telescope (JWST) is a large, space-based observatory optimized for infrared wavelengths. It is the scientific successor to the Hubble Space Telescope, and it is designed to look further into the universe than ever before, unveiling the first galaxies formed after the Big Bang and providing unprecedented insights into the formation of stars and planetary systems.
